Master’s Thesis

My master’s thesis at TSAS titled "Role of SHGs in the formation of a sustainable community: A Case Study Approach” examined the factors which lead to the formation of sustainable Self-Help Groups (SHGs) using the Social Network Analysis (SNA). Three study sites namely Kamalapur village in Gulbarga district of Karnataka, Adarshnagar village in Dholpur district of Rajasthan and Nehru Colony, an urban slum in Faridabad were selected based on the requirement of the study. The dissertation emphasized the role of SHGs in making the community sustainable by acting as a catalyst in social capital formation which increases community participation leading to personal and social empowerment. It brings out various factors affecting the formation or break-down of SHGs. It tests the following conjecture in the three locations: ‘Indirect relations exist among individuals, who help in the formation of a SHG and eventually become direct ties. Also, once the SHG is formed, an incomplete network tends towards complete network over time.’ It also analyses the factors which lead SHGs to endogenously venture into non-financial activities. The study asserts on understanding human behaviour in order to achieve developmental objectives.

Achievements

Instrumental in declaration of Dholpur district Open Defecation Free (ODF)

I was primarily working in the Dholpur district of Rajasthan as part of a fellowship programme called 'Zila Swachh Bharat Prerak’ (ZSBP) initiated by the Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation (MDWS) in collaboration with Sir Ratan Tata Trust to facilitate the implementation of the Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) at the district level under the constant guidance of the District Collector. This essentially included making the district open defecation free (ODF) and creating an overall atmosphere of cleanliness by social and behaviour change communication (SBCC) which included information, education, communication (IEC) and inter-personal communication (IPC). The most significant impact of my work had been to declare the district Open Defecation Free (ODF) which essentially means that almost all HHs have a toilet constructed and most of them understand the importance of using them.
